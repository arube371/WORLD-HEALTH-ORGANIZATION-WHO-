# WORLD-HEALTH-ORGANIZATION-WHO-
Basic Information

Full name: World Health Organization

Abbreviation: WHO

Founded: 7 April 1948 (celebrated annually as World Health Day)

Headquarters: Geneva, Switzerland

Parent organization: United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)

Current Director-General (as of 2025): Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus

 Main Objectives

The WHO’s mission is “to promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable.” Its key goals include:

Eradicating and controlling diseases (e.g., malaria, HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis)

Coordinating responses to global health emergencies (like COVID-19)

Setting international health standards and guidelines

Supporting universal health coverage (UHC)

Promoting mental health and environmental health

 Key Functions

Research and Data: Collects and analyzes global health statistics.

Standards: Publishes the International Classification of Diseases (ICD).

Policy Guidance: Advises countries on health policy and best practices.

Emergency Response: Leads efforts during health crises (e.g., Ebola, COVID-19).

Vaccination and Immunization: Runs programs like COVAX.
